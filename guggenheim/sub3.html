A dynamic, global history of ideas.

Guggenheim is actively engaged in providing its peers and the public with a nuanced understanding of modern and contemporary art

Since its founding as a modern art museum, the Guggenheim has pursued three central ideas: abstract art and its legacies, art of the present, and international perspectives on avant-garde art. Travel and research-based collection and program initiatives focused on expanding the geographic representation and interpretive frameworks of modern and contemporary art have long defined the museum’s curatorial culture. Through its exhibitions, curatorial residencies, publications, acquisitions, off-site programs, and online platforms.

Since 2006, the Guggenheim has launched a series of global art research initiatives dedicated to the study, interpretation and presentation of modern and contemporary art from beyond Europe and North America. Currently focused on Asia, the Middle East, and Latin America, these curatorial-driven initiatives examine advanced but lesser-known artistic movements and link them to expanded histories of modernism and contemporary art practices.

These developments coincide with a transformational moment

By integrating non-Western and emerging perspectives of specific meaning and context into the museum’s distinguished narrative of European and American modernism and international contemporary art, the Guggenheim advances its mission as a curatorial laboratory and dynamic cultural platform for 21st–century audiences worldwide.
